# 01. Business Case: Project HelpLocal

## 1. Problem / Opportunity
In the aftermath of recent global health shifts, **15% of our local elderly population** reports significant difficulty accessing basic groceries and medications. Currently, there is no centralized, vetted platform for them to request help safely, leading to a reliance on informal, unmonitored social media groups which pose a security risk.

## 2. Proposed Solution
**HelpLocal** is a mobile-first software application that uses a multi-factor authentication (MFA) system to verify local volunteers. It allows "Requestors" to post simple tasks (Errands) that are matched via GPS to "Volunteers" in a 5km radius.

## 3. Expected Benefits
* **Reduced Isolation:** A projected 20% increase in community interaction for housebound users.
* **Safety & Security:** All volunteers undergo a digital ID verification, reducing the risk of fraud compared to social media.
* **Efficiency:** Automated matching reduces the time to find help from 24 hours (manual) to under 30 minutes (automated).

## 4. Financial Summary (Estimated)
| Item | Cost/Value |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Estimated Development Cost** | $45,000 (MVP Phase) |
| **Operational Costs (Monthly)** | $1,200 (Server & ID Verification API) |
| **Return on Investment (ROI)** | Break-even in 12 months through local council grants and premium "Business" features. |

## 5. Risks & Mitigations
* **Risk:** Low volunteer sign-up rate. 
  * *Mitigation:* Partner with local universities to offer "Community Service Credits" to students.
* **Risk:** Data Privacy (User addresses).
  * *Mitigation:* Implement end-to-end encryption and "hidden address" features until a volunteer is accepted.
